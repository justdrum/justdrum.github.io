Personal Reflection

Upon entering the field of education, I knew there would come a time when I would need to further my education and improve my skills, not only for myself but for my students and our school. After researching different degree programs at a number of different schools, I finally landed on the University of Missouri. The Information Science and Learning Technologies department, specifically. As a career and technical education teacher, I felt this program was perfect for me with its degree emphasis on technology in schools. I felt this would help bolster my own classroom teachings as well as bring me up to speed on all the latest technologies available in education.

With the technology landscape ever-changing, I felt this degree would be a vital resource in growing our departments' reach within the school. While some teachers prefer to stay away from technology, whether it be lack of knowledge or intimidation, I welcome it with open arms. The students want to have the ability to utilize the best and most up-to-date technology, so I felt it was a necessity for myself to be as fluent as possible out of respect for them. After gaining a first-class education on technology in the classroom, I feel well equipped to guide my students through use of technology in the education.

Prior to beginning this program in September of 2020, I was very limited in my knowledge of tools such as Canvas and Google Apps, not to mention my knowledge in game design and implementation to the classroom. I would definitely have labeled myself a novice in the areas of digital media in general, and limited to only entry-level knowledge with things such and audio and digital creativity. Through this program, I have expanded my knowledge not only in those fields, but also in things such as research skills, basic html and css work along with many other new found skills.

After having been in the program for over a year now, I find myself very aware of what media is available and how to utilize it to its fullest extent within my classroom.
